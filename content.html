Research Experience

As an IPI Cybersecurity Research Fellow, I focused my efforts on investigating the intersection of international governance and international cybersecurity governance. From my research, I wrote a paper titled "Intersecting Frontiers: International Governance in Environmentalism and Cybersecurity." It explores how we might adapt mechanisms from international environmentalism to develop a better framework for international cyber security governance. Through this paper, I hope to contribute to developing a more robust and comprehensive approach to international cybersecurity governance. Through the Global Research Group, and under the leadership of Dr. Jessica Beyer, our team collaborated with the National Democratic Institute (NDI) to undertake a comprehensive research initiative. We compiled research and wrote a report on the landscape of ransomware and cybercrime trends, spanning 46 countries within six diverse regions where NDI works. My particular focus within this project was delving into international multi-stakeholder forums dedicated to addressing cybercrime and cybersecurity efforts for governments. I concentrated on understanding these forums' intricate mechanisms and capacity-building approaches. This experience expanded my knowledge and allowed me to contribute to a critical international dialogue on cyber threats and global security.
